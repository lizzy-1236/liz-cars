History of sedan

The term "sedan" has its origins in Italy and derives from the Latin words "sedes" or "sedere." It was first used in reference to an automobile body in 1912. The concept of a sedan comes from the sedan chair, a one-person enclosed box with windows that was carried by porters in the 17th century12. Sedans were originally highly luxurious and elaborately decorated in Italy, France, and England during the 17th and 18th centuries
